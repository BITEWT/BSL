PRINCIPLE OF GRAPHIC DESIGN

GRAPHIC

Graphic design is also closely related to emotional design, as designers must consider the emotions, expectations and beliefs of the viewer when creating images. This is where color theory comes into play, as the chosen color palette not only conveys certain messages to the viewer, but also influences their emotional response to the design. For example, red is a color often seen as a symbol of danger or alarm, while blue is associated with the sea and sky and therefore encourages a calmer response.

DESIGN

Graphic design plays an important role in the way consumers interact with, perceive and experience your brand. Therefore, it is important that you not only understand the practice but also implement it in your company. Additionally, the elements and principles of graphic design are applied to communicate specific messages, establish brand identity, attract target audiences, and improve user experience. All of this is necessary for your business to not only survive, but thrive.

LOGO

Your logo differentiates your company from its competitors and is the most important design element that represents your brand. It is often the first thing a target customer sees regarding your business and therefore has the opportunity to make a good first impression and convince them to do business with you. While typography plays a crucial role in the success of your logo, the color palette also plays a crucial role. Graphic design uses color theory to ensure that your logo's color palette conveys the right message to the right audience and evokes the emotions you want them to feel when interacting with your brand.
